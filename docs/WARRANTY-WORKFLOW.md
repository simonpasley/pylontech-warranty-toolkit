# Warranty workflow

How to take a diagnostic from this toolkit through to a Pylontech RMA.

> **Disclaimer**: Warranty terms and processes vary by region, distributor, installation date and model. This guide reflects observed UK practice in 2025–26. It is not legal or contractual advice. Always check your own paperwork and speak to your distributor. The author accepts zero liability — see the project [LICENSE](../LICENSE).

---

## Step 1 — Confirm the pack is actually failing

Before raising a warranty claim, run a full diagnostic on the suspect pack with this toolkit. The verdict logic is conservative: if the pack genuinely is failing, the toolkit's verdict will be **DEGRADING** or **FAILED**. If the verdict comes back **HEALTHY**, the issue is elsewhere (inverter, cabling, configuration, settings) and a warranty claim will be rejected on inspection.

Things to capture:

- The full warranty report (Markdown file) generated by the "Download warranty report" button
- The full event-log dump from the **"Dump full event log"** button (32 most recent stored events)
- Optionally the longer event-history dump (**"Dump full event history"**) which walks every stored item with per-cell snapshots
- **Photos** of every failed pack: front face, RJ45 connectors, terminal block, label/barcode, any signs of swelling or blue residue at ports

For maximum-strength evidence on a specific pack, plug the cable **directly into that pack's console port** rather than via the master — you will get authoritative per-cell SOH counts and a complete event log local to that pack.

## Step 2 — Find your warranty term

Pylontech US3000C / US5000 carry a manufacturer warranty period (commonly **7–10 years** depending on model, region and registration status) from the date of installation. To check whether your pack is in warranty, you need:

| | |
|---|---|
| Barcode | Read off the case label (or the toolkit's `info` command) — Pylontech format is one letter (e.g. `H` for US3000C, `Y` for US5000) followed by a date code and unit serial |
| Production date | Either on the case label, or BMS `Release Date` field (these are usually close to each other) |
| Installation date | From your installer's commissioning paperwork |

A pack manufactured in late 2021 will, in 2026, be ~4 years old — comfortably inside any current Pylontech warranty term.

## Step 3 — Contact route

### UK installations

**Pylontech UK service team**: `service.uk@pylontech.com.cn`

This address is publicly listed by Pylontech UK and is the route for warranty enquiries from UK installers. Phone numbers and contact details may change — check the current details on the Pylontech UK website or your installer's records.

### Other regions

Contact your distributor first. Most regions have a dedicated Pylontech service partner who handles RMA logistics in that country.

## Step 4 — The Diagnosis Form

Pylontech UK typically sends a "Diagnosis Form" template (a simple Word / PDF document) to be filled in for each unit being claimed. The form asks for:

| Section | Fields |
|---|---|
| Contact information | Name, email, phone, shipping address |
| Battery information | Position (M / S 1 / S 2 …), serial / barcode, model, production date, alarm state, blue residue / ingress in ports, battery voltage |
| Installation information | Install date, installation company, inverter make and model, installation address |
| Fault information | Description of fault, fault codes on inverter, fault codes on BatteryView, CAN pin configuration |
| Photos | Full front of each battery and connections, plus any swelling photos |
| Disclaimer | A signature acknowledging that out-of-warranty packs incur collection and maintenance costs |

The toolkit's warranty report contains the technical content the form asks about. Fill in the Diagnosis Form for each pack, and attach the toolkit's report and the event-log dumps as supporting evidence.

---

## Step 5 — What to attach

For each failed pack, send:

1. **Completed Pylontech Diagnosis Form** (one per unit)
2. **Warranty report** from this toolkit (`pylontech-report-<barcode>.md`)
3. **Full event-log dump** from this toolkit (`pylontech-eventlog-<barcode>-<date>.txt`)
4. **Photos** — front of each pack, terminal block, label, any swelling, clean install context shot

If you have multiple packs failing in the same rack from the same manufacturing batch (sequential serial numbers, identical release date), call this out explicitly in your covering email — Pylontech RMA teams treat batch defects as a single coherent case rather than N independent ones.

---

## Step 6 — Collection and turnaround

If the claim is accepted, Pylontech UK typically arranges a **courier collection** (Dachser is one common carrier in the UK) of the failed pack(s) at no cost to the installer. Typical turnaround once collected: a few weeks to a couple of months for inspection, RMA approval and replacement dispatch, depending on lab capacity.

Keep your case email thread alive — chase politely if you do not hear updates after 4–6 weeks.

---

## Step 7 — Replacement vs repair

Pylontech may either:

- **Replace** the unit outright with a new (or warranty-stock) unit of the same model
- **Repair** by swapping internal modules (CCMU / PMU / CMU boards, individual cells) and returning the same unit

Both are normal warranty outcomes. If you have a strong preference, mention it in your initial email — but the decision usually rests with Pylontech.

---

## Step 8 — While you wait

A pack pulled from a rack pending warranty inspection should be:

- **Stored at moderate state of charge** (around 50 %) — not full, not empty
- **Disconnected from any inverter**
- **In a cool, dry, ventilated area** — not in direct sunlight, not in damp areas, not in a car boot, not in a corner with no airflow
- **Away from flammable materials** — the cell may be failing further; treat with respect

Lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) cells are the safer chemistry within lithium, but failed packs deserve the same careful storage as any other lithium battery awaiting return.

---

## Things this toolkit does **not** do

- Negotiate with Pylontech on your behalf
- Guarantee a successful warranty outcome
- Replace the official Diagnosis Form (use both)
- Cover packs that are out of warranty (those become a question for [ServTec](https://servtec.co.uk/) or another paid Pylontech repair service)
- Diagnose physical damage (swelling, leaking electrolyte, corroded contacts) — always inspect the pack physically as well

---

## Honest summary

This toolkit reads what the BMS says about itself. It produces clear, authoritative evidence in a format Pylontech RMA teams can verify against their own records. It will not magic a warranty out of nothing — but if a pack is genuinely failing within its warranty term, the evidence this toolkit produces should make the case clear-cut.

Good luck with your claim.
